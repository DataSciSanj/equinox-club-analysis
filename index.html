Data Description and Collection

Equinox Data

Equinox fitness, part of the Equinox Group, is a subsidiary of privately-held real estate firm The Related Companies. Internal data such as same-store revenues, membership, retention, etc. are not publicly available. The limitations presented by the lack of such data to a more thorough analysis are discussed below. The scope of Equinox-related data used in this analysis is restricted to location information available on their website [www.equinox.com/clubs]. This provided the names, addresses, phone numbers, and amenities for each club. Of particular note for analytical purposes, was the inclusion of ZIP codes, which were used to join club records to external data. Equinox club location data was collected with a web scraping script built using the BeautifulSoup library in Python. This resulted in an initial Equinox location dataset of 114 clubs.

Data Cleaning and Preparation

For this analysis, the initial data cleaning steps involved handling missing values in the eqnx_with_census_data.csv file. We began by filtering out non-US records, which were identified by missing values in the State column. This was necessary because the analysis focuses exclusively on US locations.

We then addressed missing values in numeric columns using a median imputation strategy:

  • If a Location has 5 or more sites, the median of that specific location was used to fill the missing values.
  • If a Location has fewer than 5 sites, the median of the entire column was used.

After these steps, the dataset was cleaned and ready for further analysis, with non-numeric NaNs preserved for potential categorical analysis.

Correlation Analysis: Key Factors

We performed a correlation analysis to identify the strongest relationships between demographic and economic factors at Equinox locations. The following insights were derived:

  • Household Costs and Family Income: Strong correlations were observed between household costs (both for renters and homeowners) and family income, indicating that Equinox locations are typically found in wealthier areas.
  • Population and Family Size: The population count is strongly correlated with the number of families and individuals, suggesting that Equinox locations are in denser, family-oriented areas.
  • Household Occupancy and Income: Higher household occupancy rates are associated with higher incomes, further supporting the idea that Equinox locations are in affluent neighborhoods.

Hypothesis Testing: Median Income

To determine if the median income at Equinox locations is significantly different from the national median income, we conducted a Mann-Whitney U test. This non-parametric test was chosen due to the lack of variance in the national income data, which made a t-test unreliable.

Mann-Whitney U Test Results:

  • U-Statistic: 99.0
  • P-Value: 0.1547

The p-value indicates that there is no statistically significant difference between the median income at Equinox locations and the national median income. This suggests that while Equinox locations are in wealthier areas, the difference is not statistically significant at the 5% level.

Hypothesis Testing: Median Income

To determine if the median income at Equinox locations is significantly different from the national median income, we initially conducted an independent t-test. The goal was to assess whether the observed differences in income were statistically significant.

However, the t-test revealed issues due to the lack of variance in the national median income data, leading to unreliable results. Specifically, the national income data showed very little variation, which made the t-test unsuitable for this analysis.

To address this, we chose to use the Mann-Whitney U test, a non-parametric alternative that does not assume a normal distribution and is better suited for comparing medians when distributions differ significantly. This choice was made to ensure the robustness of the statistical analysis, providing a more accurate assessment of the differences in median income.

Mann-Whitney U Test Results:

  • U-Statistic: 99.0
  • P-Value: 0.1547

The p-value indicates that there is no statistically significant difference between the median income at Equinox locations and the national median income. This suggests that while Equinox locations are in wealthier areas, the difference is not statistically significant at the 5% level.
