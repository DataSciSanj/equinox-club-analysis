EQUINOX Market Analysis

Introduction & Objective

We analyze demographic, economic, and housing characteristics of current Equinox club locations to identify patterns that could inform future site selection. Despite not having access to internal usage data, the goal is to demonstrate how external data might provide insights into potential new locations.

Data Description and Collection

Equinox Data

Equinox fitness, part of the Equinox Group, is a subsidiary of privately-held real estate firm The Related Companies. Internal data such as same-store revenues, membership, retention, etc., are not publicly available. The limitations presented by the lack of such data to a more thorough analysis are discussed below.

The scope of Equinox-related data used in this analysis is restricted to location information available on their website. This provided the names, addresses, phone numbers, and amenities for each club. Of particular note for analytical purposes, was the inclusion of ZIP codes, which were used to join club records to external data.

Equinox club location data was collected with a web scraping script built using the BeautifulSoup library in Python, resulting in an initial Equinox location dataset of 114 clubs.

Equinox Club Locations - Summary and Focus

After collecting the data on Equinox club locations, we found the following key statistics:

  • Total Number of Clubs: 114
  • Number of Clubs in the UK: 3
  • Number of Clubs in Canada: 3
  • Number of Clubs in the US: 108
  • Number of Location Regions in the US: 13

For our analysis, we focus exclusively on the clubs in the United States, where there are 108 clubs across 14 states, 56 cities, and 94 zip codes. These clubs will be examined as model profiles to glean insights into the demographic, economic, and household data of the areas they are located in.

External Data/Market Data/Demo, Econ, Housing/Socio-Economic Data

Data pertaining to the demographic, economic, income, and housing characteristics of locations where Equinox clubs are located were sourced from the U.S. Census. This analysis focuses on the most recent year's (2022) demographic, income, and housing data estimates.

The following datasets were primarily leveraged in the analysis presented here:

  • American Community Survey 2022: 5-Year Estimates S0101 | Age and Sex
  • American Community Survey 2022: 5-Year Estimates S1903 | Median Income in the Past 12 Months
  • American Community Survey 2022: 5-Year Estimates S2503 | Financial Characteristics

These files were downloaded directly from the Census Bureau's data website after being filtered to ensure the output files provided ZIP code level data.

Data Cleaning and Preparation

For this analysis, the initial data cleaning steps involved handling missing values in the eqnx_with_census_data.csv file. We began by filtering out non-US records and addressing missing values in numeric columns using a median imputation strategy.

After these steps, the dataset was cleaned and ready for further analysis, with non-numeric NaNs preserved for potential categorical analysis.

Correlation Analysis: Key Factors

We performed a correlation analysis to identify the strongest relationships between demographic and economic factors at Equinox locations. The following insights were derived:

  • Household Costs and Family Income: Strong correlations were observed between household costs and family income, indicating that Equinox locations are typically found in wealthier areas.
  • Population and Family Size: The population count is strongly correlated with the number of families and individuals, suggesting that Equinox locations are in denser, family-oriented areas.
  • Household Occupancy and Income: Higher household occupancy rates are associated with higher incomes, further supporting the idea that Equinox locations are in affluent neighborhoods.

Hypothesis Testing: Median Income

To determine if the median income at Equinox locations is significantly different from the national median income, we conducted a Mann-Whitney U test. This non-parametric test was chosen due to the lack of variance in the national income data, which made a t-test unreliable.

Mann-Whitney U Test Results:

  • U-Statistic: 99.0
  • P-Value: 0.1547

The p-value indicates that there is no statistically significant difference between the median income at Equinox locations and the national median income.
