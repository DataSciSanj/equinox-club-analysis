Summary of the data cleaning steps taken: imputation of missing values, standardization, and normalization of data where necessary. Any challenges encountered during data cleaning.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Histograms of Key Numerical Variables

Analysis of the distributions for median ages (both male and female), household income, owned household income, and monthly housing costs.

Insights: Median household income for owned homes shows a spread towards higher income ranges, indicating a higher economic bracket for homeowners in these areas. Renters' median household income is more clustered towards the lower end, suggesting a different economic demographic compared to homeowners.

Geographical Analysis

State-Level Income Analysis: Median household income, owned household income, and renter household income were analyzed across states.

Key Observations: Certain states like IL and NY have higher median owned household incomes, suggesting these might be key markets for Equinox. Renter household income shows less variability across states but highlights the economic differences in renting vs. owning a home.

Age and Income Relationship

Correlation Analysis: Weak correlations were found between age (both male and female) and income variables, suggesting that age is not a strong predictor of income in these regions.

Potential Future Analyses

Cluster Analysis: Can analyze the clusters in more detail by examining the distribution of other features such as household income, occupied household income, and owned household income across different clusters.

We might also want to explore different pairs of features to see if other combinations reveal more distinct patterns between clusters. Exploring the clustering results in the context of geographical differences or age and income relationships might also yield interesting insights.

Limitations

External Data Constraints: The analysis was limited to publicly available data. Internal Equinox data on memberships, revenue, and specific marketing campaigns could provide deeper insights.

Geographical Granularity: While state-level analysis was insightful, more detailed city or neighborhood-level data would allow for more precise targeting and understanding of local markets.

Time-Series Analysis: A longitudinal analysis could reveal trends over time, but this was outside the scope due to data limitations.

Conclusion

Summary of key findings and their implications for Equinox.
